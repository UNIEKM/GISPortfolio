Cartography

This map provides an overview of the distribution of wildland fires in Canada, specifically focusing on fires that have grown to a final size of 200 hectares or greater. Canada is home to 9% of the world's forests, and it has been estimated that an average of 2.5 million hectares are burned annually. While only 3% of all wildland fires exceed 200 hectares in size, they account for a staggering 97% of the total area burned in the country. Despite wildland fires being a natural occurrence within forest ecosystems, their impact on the economy cannot be overlooked, with annual fire suppression costs ranging from 800 million to 1.5 billion over the last decade.

Principal Sources:

Base map land relief derived from Digital Elevation Model acquired through USGS Earth Explorer (earthexplorer.usgs.gov), EROS Archive-Digital Elevation-Global 30 Arc-Second Elevation (GTOPO30), Entity ID GT30W140N90 (1996-12-01).

Seafloor relief derived from GEBCO Gridded Bathymetry Data Download (2022) on a 15 Arc-Second geographic latitude and longitude grid (download.gebco.net). GEBCO operates under the joint auspices of the International Hydrographic Organization (IHO) and the Intergovernmental Oceanographic Commission (IOC) (UNESCO).

Ocean, Lake, River, Mountain, Glaciated Area, Populated places, Political boundaries and Railroads data derived from Natural Earth 1:10m (2022) (www.naturalearthdata.com).

Canada National Highway System Data derived from the Government of Canada/Transport Canada (2021) (open.canada.ca).

Large Fire Perimeter data from the Canadian Forest Service (2021), Canadian National Fire Database-Agency Fire Data, Natural Resources Canada, Canadian Forest Service, Northern Forestry Centre, Edmonton, Alberta (cwfis.cfs.nrcan.gc.ca/ha/nfdb).
